Conceptual Book Cover

I was tasked with creating three different book cover variations for the same classic novel. The three cover variations were classified as:

Typography Cover - A cover that solely uses typography to convey the meaning or message behind the book.

Handmade Cover - A cover that is made from physical materials.

Designer's Choice Cover - A cover that could be developed in any style or format as long as it employs a creative design strategy.

My project was based on "The Scarlet Pimpernel" by Baroness Emmuska Orczy which was printed in 1905 after originally being produced as a play in 1903. When I was first sketching designs, I wanted the typography cover to closely resemble designs found in the early 1900's printing styles. I was ecstatic when I found a textured mockup as well.

I included some of the visual experiments I made during the design process before settling on the final designs.

Type Poster

The goal of this project was to create a poster for a typeface of our choosing to be used as an advertisement showing off its specific qualities. This poster would then be used to entice other designers to purchase the font, in this case, from fonts.com.

The poster includes background information about the typeface, the original designer Giambattisa Bodoni, and an example of the different weights offered.

It's always hard narrowing down to a final design choice. I enjoy the creative process that leads to a final product.

Business Card

I was tasked with creating a business card for my own brand that could be used professionally and focused solely on typography.

By using design principles and a strict grid system, I was able to achieve a professional and elegant business card with a hint of whimsy.

I had a lot of fun coming up with different business card designs and played with several different styles.

Theater Poster

The goal of this project was to create symbol and typography posters for a Shakespeare theater production. I chose the play, Macbeth.

The first typography poster captures Macbeth's descent into madness after committing the murders.

For the symbol poster, I converted a photograph I took into an image trace.

The second typography poster is an ambigram I designed for the quote ‘Fair is Foul and Foul is Fair’.

Pamphlet

The purpose of this assignment was to create a pamphlet that could be gifted focusing on a talk of our choosing from the October 2022 LDS General Conference.

Typography and layout design were the most important aspects of this unit. We were to use as little graphic influences as possible so all of the attention could be on the different typographical components and the message being presented.

I chose to use Elder D. Todd Christofferson's talk titled The Doctrine of Belonging.

Ad Campaign - Nonliteral

This project involved creating a non-literal magazine ad for a randomly generated household item. My object was toilet paper.

I immediately thought of the Charmin toilet paper brand with its catchy jingles and oh-so-soft bath tissue.

Logo Design

When this project was assigned, I immediately fell in love. The purpose was to develop a logo for a fictional shop of our choice with an animal mascot.

I created the fictional boba tea shop ‘Bubble Panda’ and decided to use a Red Panda as my mascot. This project was so much fun and I had an absolute blast designing this logo.

Ad Campaign - Existing

This ad was created to mimic an existing ad campaign (slide 2).

I chose to emulate Barilla Pasta because I love the creativity and playfulness of their seasonal ads.
